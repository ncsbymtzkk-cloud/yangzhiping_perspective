## How to Read This Document

Each decision is structured as:
- **Context**: What was happening at the time
- **The Decision**: What he actually did
- **Stated Reasoning**: His own explanation (verbatim or paraphrased from his writings)
- **Observer's Lens**: What an outside analyst might note
- **Outcome**: What happened as a result
- **Post-hoc Reflection**: How he later characterized the decision
- **Confidence**: How well-documented the decision is (High/Medium/Low)

## 1. From Psychology Graduate to Competitive Intelligence Analyst (circa 2003)

### Context
Yang Zhiping graduated from Beijing Normal University (psychological measurement major). His first job out of college was at 清华紫光 (Tsinghua Ziguang), where he was hired to work in a new psychology department funded by Tsinghua Ziguang to support Dr. Xu Haoyuan (徐浩渊), a student and friend of Aaron Beck (founder of CBT). He was the only male employee in the department and was tasked with developing an online cognitive behavioral therapy system.

### The Decision
After falling ill and leaving the CBT project, he accepted a position at another Tsinghua-affiliated company doing **enterprise competitive intelligence** consulting -- work far from his psychology background.

### Stated Reasoning
- His illness forced him to leave the psychology project
- He needed income, so he did project work for a psychology institute professor
- The professor helped him find a new job at a Tsinghua-affiliated enterprise
- The original plan was to co-found a human resources consulting division; the division never materialized
- He joined the existing competitive intelligence team instead

### Observer's Lens
This is the first example of Yang's career being shaped by **serendipity and contingency** rather than deliberate planning -- a pattern he would later theorize in his embrace of Ibarra's "possible selves" theory and "act first, plan later" approach to career transitions. A psychology graduate ending up in intelligence analysis was not strategic; it was opportunistic.

### Outcome
He spent years doing competitive intelligence work, writing reports, searching commercial databases, conducting field research. His first project involved tracking R&D and investment intelligence of Fortune 500 companies in China.

### Post-hoc Reflection
Writing 20 years later (2020), Yang framed this as foundational: "If 20 years ago, fresh out of school, I had stubbornly clung to my identity as a psychology graduate and treated the competitive intelligence work as something to just get through, I would most likely have missed the opportunity to improve my information analysis skills."

He traces his 2018 course "信息分析" directly back to this early training. In 2020, he had 开智学堂 join the Chinese Competitive Intelligence Association.

### Confidence
**High**. Documented in his own detailed account (yangzhiping.com and caixin blog, 2020).

## 2. Founding 安人测评 (Anren Assessment) -- First Entrepreneurship (2003)

### Context
After working in competitive intelligence and management consulting, Yang founded a psychological measurement company. He had prior experience developing psychological assessment systems during and after college.

### The Decision
Founded 安人测评 (also known as 京湘科技心理学研究中心), a company providing psychological and educational measurement services to educational institutions, medical institutions, and military organizations.

### Stated Reasoning
- He saw a market gap for professional psychological assessment tools in China
- His background in psychological measurement made this a natural fit
- Early projects were meaningful: after the Ma Jiajue incident, his team was contracted to build psychological profiles for 100,000 university students in Yunnan within one month to identify high-risk individuals

### Outcome
The company achieved moderate success, developing assessment systems used across many provinces and institutions. It also played a role in the post-Wenchuan earthquake psychological relief effort in 2008.

### Post-hoc Reflection
Yang later wrote that the Wenchuan earthquake experience in 2008 was transformative: "In the face of life and death, my values were greatly changed." This directly led to his next major decision.

### Confidence
**High**. Documented on yangzhiping.com/about/ and in his essay "成为自己，才是对自己的奖赏" (2019).

## 3. Selling Company Shares, Leaving Business, Becoming a Hermit Writer (2010)

### Context
After the 2008 Wenchuan earthquake psychological relief effort, Yang experienced a value shift. He was running 安人测评 but felt a deep disconnect.

### The Decision
Transferred his company shares, quit his job, left the commercial world entirely, and became a full-time independent writer. He spent over four years (2010-2014) reading and writing in Beijing and his hometown, away from business.

### Stated Reasoning
- The Wenchuan earthquake experience changed his values profoundly
- He wanted to pursue writing and thinking rather than business management
- He wanted to "become himself" -- to be an intrinsically motivated person rather than one driven by external rewards

### Observer's Lens
This is Yang's most radical identity shift: from entrepreneur to reclusive writer. It lasted 4+ years. He frames this as value-driven rather than failure-driven -- 安人测评 was not failing when he left. This period of withdrawal is crucial to his self-narrative: it is when he read deeply, developed his thinking, and built foundations for what came later.

### Outcome
He wrote extensively, developed his personal knowledge system, and built the intellectual foundations for what would become 开智学堂, his books, and his 元学教育体系.

### Note on Timeline Ambiguity
There is a tension: Yang says he "left the commercial world" in 2010, but he also "founded 安人心智" in the same year. This may mean his exit was partial, or that 安人心智 was initially a small side project. The "4 years of hermit writing" may have been a phased transition rather than a clean break.

### Confidence
**Medium-High**. Described in multiple essays but with some timeline ambiguity.

## 4. Founding 安人心智 (Anren Mind Group) -- Second Entrepreneurship (2010)

### Context
After his value-driven withdrawal, Yang founded a new company in the emerging cognitive science space.

### The Decision
Founded 安人心智 (Anren Mind Group), a brain and cognitive science company with multiple sub-brands:
- **爱贝睿 (iBabyRay)**: Children's brain science and early education
- **开智学堂 (OpenMind Club)**: Adult education for knowledge workers
- Later: Elderly cognitive health products

### Stated Reasoning
- Cognitive science was becoming increasingly prominent in China (e.g., Peking University renamed its psychology department to "School of Psychological and Cognitive Sciences" in 2016)
- He saw opportunities to apply cognitive science across the full lifespan
- He wanted to combine his psychology background with his entrepreneurial experience

### Outcome
安人心智 became the parent company for all his subsequent ventures. He remains its Chairman ("现任安人心智董事长").

### Confidence
**High**. Documented on his official bio page.

## 5. Creating 开智书友会 (Kaizhi Book Club) (2014)

### Context
In 2014, still in his "hermit writer" phase, Yang began organizing social activities around reading.

### The Decision
Created 开智书友会, a WeChat-based book club that invited over 100 "big V" friends from his network. Each member was required to share expertise through a thematic presentation.

### Stated Reasoning
- He needed a community of like-minded readers
- Simple format: share good books, invite interesting people
- A robot named "一休" (Yixiu) was built to automatically compile members' conversations into e-books sent nightly via email

### Outcome
The book club was unusually successful. Members' chat transcripts were compiled into the book 《追时间的人》, published by CITIC Press, which won Douban's "Best Book of the Year" award.
The club spawned:
- 开智青年群 (Kaizhi Youth Group, Jan 2015)
- Demand for courses leading to 开智学堂
- 开智大会 (Kaizhi Conference), inspired by TED

### Post-hoc Reflection
"Looking back now, 开智十年 seems like a carefully planned project. However, it was not designed in advance. Even after ten years, we only found a term to accurately describe what we had been doing -- 开智元学教育体系."

### Confidence
**High**. Extensively documented in "开智十年，谢谢你" (2025).

## 6. Founding 开智学堂 as a Company (2015)

### Context
The 开智书友会 grew organically. Youth group members clamored for formal courses. By February 2015, Yang sensed it was time to formalize.

### The Decision
Registered 开智学堂 as a formal company. The first course was Python Programming, taught by an expert from the book club. Yang personally taught the second course, 认知写作学 (Cognitive Writing).

### Stated Reasoning
- Community demand for courses was clear and growing
- He felt a "premonition" that formal registration was necessary
- He disagreed with approaches taken by other education entrepreneurs, finding them inefficient
- "I didn't know what exactly I was going to do. I only knew that I didn't agree with the path some friends were taking."

### Observer's Lens
The "act first, plan later" pattern is striking. 开智学堂 was founded without a clear curriculum philosophy. The philosophy (元学教育) was retroactively constructed over a decade. This is consistent with his own advice about career transitions, but also means early customers bought into an undefined experiment.

### Outcome
开智学堂 became the core of his education ecosystem. Courses expanded from Python and Cognitive Writing to the "英才四课." It attracted thousands of students.

### Confidence
**High**. Documented in "开智十年，谢谢你" and "元学教育的若干思考."

## 7. Hosting the First 开智大会 (2015)

### Context
Book club members wanted an in-person gathering. The club happened to include several TED community organizers.

### The Decision
Organized the first 开智大会 in a TED-style immersive talk format. Speakers included 安替, who had spoken at TED Global.

### Stated Reasoning
- The community wanted offline connection
- TED-style format leveraged friends' expertise in the club
- It was a natural extension of the book club

### Outcome
Major success. The conference "broke out of its niche." The second conference benefited from speaker 郝景芳 winning the Hugo Award just before the event, amplifying reach.

### Confidence
**High**.

## 8. The 2017 Southern Tour: Pivot from Disciplinary to Meta-Skills (2017-2018)

### Context
After two years of running 开智学堂, Yang was dissatisfied with results. Courses (Python, writing, cognitive science) felt undifferentiated from what was already available.

### The Decision
In the second half of 2017, he traveled south to Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Hangzhou, and Shanghai, conducting in-depth conversations with hundreds of students. Following this tour, he fundamentally restructured 开智's curriculum from a **discipline-oriented model** to a **meta-skill model**.

### Stated Reasoning
"The most important insight was: I needed to abandon discipline-oriented curriculum design. For knowledge workers aged 18-48, they don't have time to learn economics, psychology, etc. from scratch like college students. They need knowledge and skills that can be reused across multiple domains."

### The "Epiphany"
Yang describes this as a sudden realization during a small symposium in Guangzhou: "like a divine revelation" (如同天启). The insight was to create courses in Information Analysis, Behavioral Analysis, Argument Analysis, and Decision Analysis -- the "英才四课."

### Observer's Lens
This is arguably Yang's most important intellectual turning point. It marks the transition from "teaching cognitive science" to "teaching cognitive skills." The meta-skill approach became the foundation of his entire intellectual system. However, the language of "divine revelation" is characteristic of his rhetorical style -- he frequently narrativizes decisions as moments of sudden clarity.

### Outcome
The 英才四课 became 开智's signature offerings and the foundation for his 元学教育体系.

### Post-hoc Reflection
Years later, he named this framework 开智元学教育体系: meta-methods, meta-skills, meta-knowledge, meta-cognition. He considers this his most important achievement from 2015-2025.

### Confidence
**High**. Documented in "开智十年，谢谢你" and "我为什么要研发信息分析这门课."

## 9. Disbanding 开智书友会 (November 2018)

### Context
The book club had grown to include over 100 major Chinese "big V" influencers with a combined fan base of over 100 million. Internal tensions were mounting.

### The Decision
Yang unilaterally disbanded the highly regarded 开智书友会. He did not explain his reasons at the time, and remained silent when former members asked why.

### Stated Reasoning (retrospective, 2025)
Yang later gave a remarkably candid account:
"Reasons came down to these words: 不爽 (not happy), 没意思了 (no longer interesting)."
Specific grievances:
- Takers vs. givers: A contributor asked for donations to support volunteer services; another big V immediately criticized him
- Conflict among elites: A CEO in a low period snapped at a government employee member for "chatting without taste"
- General unhappiness: The enjoyment had gone out of it

### Observer's Lens
This is one of the most revealing episodes about Yang's leadership style. He admits the decision was made "impulsively" (冲动之下). The rationale ("不爽, 没意思了") is strikingly emotional and subjective for someone who builds his brand around rationality and cognitive science. The decision dismantled a community he describes as "the gathering of China's smartest people."

### Outcome
The community was destroyed. Some members formed spin-off communities. Yang remained silent about his reasons for years.

### Post-hoc Reflection (2025)
"All the accumulated trivial frustrations boiled down to these words: not happy, no longer interesting."
In 2025, he restarted the book club under a lighter model (operating entirely on 知识星球). He described the restart as "finding new excitement" -- again emphasizing personal enjoyment over strategic necessity.

### Confidence
**High**. Detailed in "欢迎你，开智书友会的新朋友" (2025).

## 10. The Third 开智大会 and Team Implosion (2018)

### Context
The third 开智大会 was approaching. Yang's relationship with two core team members -- a product manager and a programmer -- had deteriorated.

### The Decision
He announced to his team that this might be the last 开智大会. After the conference, he was hospitalized for a week. The development team largely dissolved (most members quit). The conference was paused for five years (2018-2022).

### Stated Reasoning
"For various fortuitous reasons, young and proud, I had a falling out with two core members of the team -- a product manager and a programmer. The entire development team was nearly broken up, most resigned."
He attributed this to being "young and arrogant" (年轻气盛).

### Post-hoc Reflection
During his hospitalization, he had deep reflection:
"My true mission -- what is it? Should I be spending so much energy helping big Vs teach courses? Should I be planning the 开智大会 every year? I am clearly such an introverted person."

This hospitalization and reflection marks a key turning point. It led him to:
- Stop organizing conferences (5-year hiatus)
- Deepen curriculum development
- Shift from platform-building to direct intellectual creation

### Observer's Lens
The phrase "for various fortuitous reasons" is vague. Yang never specifies what the actual conflict was about, nor does he take substantive responsibility beyond saying he was "young and arrogant." The lack of detail makes it difficult to evaluate fault. What is clear: the team breakup was personally devastating (hospitalization) and forced a strategic reorientation.

### Confidence
**High**. Documented in "开智十年，谢谢你" (2025).

## 11. Creating the "英才四课" (2018-2022)

### Context
After the team breakup and conference hiatus, Yang doubled down on curriculum development. Between 2018 and 2022, he single-handedly developed four major courses.

### The Decision
Created the "英才四课": Information Analysis (2018), Behavioral Analysis (2021), Argument Analysis, Decision Analysis. These became the core of 开智's offering.

### Stated Reasoning
- Knowledge workers need cross-domain reusable skills, not disciplinary knowledge
- These four areas represent the most critical meta-skills for the 21st century
- He had relevant background from his competitive intelligence days and psychology training

### Outcome
Yang considers these his most significant intellectual contribution. In 2022, he declared: "With one person's effort, I built a new intelligence system, psychological system, logic system, and decision-making system."

### Observer's Lens
The claim "with one person's effort" is characteristic of Yang's self-presentation. He rarely acknowledges collaborators in his intellectual output. While he likely did most of the conceptual work, the courses were delivered with support teams.

### Confidence
**High**.

## 12. Proposing "人生发展学" (Life Development Studies) (2021-2022)

### Context
By 2021-2022, Yang had been building his intellectual system for years. He decided to formalize it into a new discipline.

### The Decision
Proposed "人生发展学" (Life Development Studies), described as "a scientific framework for measuring and encoding life development." He also launched a parallel consulting system (人生发展咨询).

### Stated Reasoning
- Existing psychological, career, and relationship counseling operate in silos
- A holistic approach to human development is needed
- He had the intellectual foundation after decades of reading and practice

### Observer's Lens
Creating a new "discipline" is an ambitious intellectual move. Yang positions himself as a system-builder. He acknowledges: "At first, almost all my friends thought I was joking."

### Outcome
By 2022, he had trained 14 consultants and completed over 100 client cases. The consulting system continues.

### Confidence
**High**.

## 13. Declaring Intent to Build "道统、学统、文统" (2023)

### Context
At age 30, Yang reportedly made a private vow: "to become a thinker like my hometown predecessor Wang Chuanshan, leaving a heavy mark on human civilization." In 2023, he announced he was beginning to fulfill this vow.

### The Decision
Publicly declared that 2023 marked the beginning of establishing his "three traditions":
- **道统** (Dao Orthodoxy): Began with a 2023 New Year's Eve lecture
- **学统** (Scholarly Tradition): Four Analyses, Four Methods, Life Development Studies
- **文统** (Literary Tradition): Cognitive Writing, published essay collections, upcoming poetry

### Stated Reasoning
- He turned 42 in 2023 -- a milestone
- His books had reached a wide audience
- His system felt mature enough to present systematically

### Observer's Lens
This is perhaps Yang's most ambitious self-positioning. He is explicitly comparing his project to great Neo-Confucian system-builders. "道统" is a term with heavy historical/Confucian baggage. To supporters, this is intellectual ambition; to critics, grandiosity.

### Outcome
Ongoing. He has published "心智三部曲," his reading system, and continues to give lectures.

### Confidence
**High**. Documented in his 2023 reading notes (2024 publication).

## 14. AI Pivot: Founding AI Community and Products (2023)

### Context
In March 2023, Yang wrote a widely-shared article about GPT. This rapidly changed his work rhythm.

### The Decision
Pivoted aggressively into AI:
- Founded a 2000+ member AI community
- Launched GPT101 general education course
- Initiated an "AI Productivity Leap" program
- Developed AI courses, workshops, and 10+ AI products
- Open-sourced projects that gained GitHub traction

### Stated Reasoning
- AI was a transformative wave he could not ignore
- He saw an opportunity to apply his cognitive science framework to AI literacy
- His community expected him to have a perspective

### Observer's Lens
Yang's AI pivot mirrors the pattern of many Chinese knowledge entrepreneurs in 2023. The timing and scope suggest opportunism alongside genuine interest. His earlier framework (元学教育) was not AI-specific, but he retroactively integrated AI into it.

### Outcome
AI products and courses reached significant user numbers. Some projects gained open-source attention.

### Confidence
**High**. Documented in his 2023 reading notes.

## 15. Restarting 开智书友会 (2025)

### Context
After seven years of silence, Yang decided to bring back the book club he had impulsively destroyed.

### The Decision
Restarted 开智书友会 with a deliberately lighter model: operating entirely through 知识星球 (Knowledge Planet), with fewer features.

### Stated Reasoning
- His books on reading had been well-received, creating a natural reason to rebuild a reading community
- The 开智 ecosystem had matured (2023's 4th conference showed community cohesion)
- He wanted a lower-barrier entry point for his ideas
- Goal: "spend 10 years deeply studying 1000 general education books"

### Learns from the Failure
Yang explicitly addressed lessons:
- "做减法" (do less): The 2024 beta period still had too many features; he cut further before launch
- "避免当年问题" (avoid past problems): The lighter model on 知识星球 reduces management burden and conflict potential

### Observer's Lens
Yang treats his own past decisions with remarkable candor. He admits the 2018 dissolution was "impulsive" and the reasons emotional. The restart shows both the value of the asset and his willingness to iterate on past failures. The reliance on a third-party platform rather than his own technology represents a retreat from his earlier vision of building proprietary community infrastructure.

### Confidence
**High**. Detailed in the 2025 announcement.

## Cross-Cutting Themes and Contradictions

### 1. "Act First, Plan Later" vs. System-Building

Yang explicitly advocates for "act before planning" in career transitions, and his own life reflects this. Major ventures (安人测评, 安人心智, 开智学堂) were started without fully formed plans. The 元学教育体系 was retroactively constructed over a decade.

**Tension**: This pragmatism coexists with grand ambition to build an enduring intellectual system (道统/学统/文统). The gap between "figuring it out as I go" and "building for the ages" is not necessarily contradictory but is notable.

### 2. Individualism vs. Community

Yang's philosophy emphasizes "我大于我们" (I is greater than we), yet his entire business model depends on community: book club, conferences, cohort courses, consulting network. He repeatedly builds communities and then withdraws (disbanding the book club, pausing the conference, retreating to write).

**Tension**: The introvert who must perform extroversion; the individualist who needs community. Yang explicitly acknowledges this: "I am clearly such an introverted person."

### 3. Anti-Commercial Posture While Running a Business

Yang presents himself as skeptical of commercialism, championing freelancers and "small beautiful companies" over venture-backed hypergrowth. Yet 安人心智 is a for-profit corporate group. 开智学堂 charges for courses.

**Tension**: His anti-commercial posture sits alongside running a commercial operation.

### 4. Openness to Criticism vs. Dismissiveness of Critics

Yang recommends "正反上下，古今中外" as a reading methodology -- always seek counter-evidence. However, when his own book is criticized, he categorizes readers into: (1) malicious misreaders who fabricate facts to attack him, and (2) readers whose cognitive ability is insufficient to understand him.

**Tension**: The advocate of "reverse thinking" seems less receptive when it targets his own work. The framing of critics as either malicious or cognitively limited insulates his ideas from challenge.

### 5. Spontaneity vs. Deliberate Image Management

Yang presents many decisions as spontaneous (impulsive book club dissolution, "divine revelation" curriculum pivot, not knowing what he was doing when starting 开智). Yet his public communications are highly crafted, with consistent branding across platforms.

**Tension**: The spontaneous decision-maker is also the meticulous image-builder.

## Key Unanswered Questions

1. **What was the actual conflict with the two core team members in 2018?** Yang attributes it to "young and arrogant" but gives no specifics.

2. **How financially successful are his ventures?** He mentions a "10 million RMB VC investment" in 2015, but no public information exists about 安人心智's profitability, revenue, or valuation.

3. **What is the actual student outcome data?** Yang provides anecdotal success stories but no published metrics on completion rates, satisfaction, or longitudinal outcomes.

4. **How original is his intellectual system?** He synthesizes from cognitive science, psychology, linguistics, etc. The question of genuine originality vs. skillful curation is inherently difficult to assess.
